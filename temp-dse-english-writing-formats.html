Formal — unknown recipient
Dear Sir or Madam, To Whom It May Concern, Dear Members of the Committee, Dear Editor,
Formal — known title / role
Dear Mr / Ms [Surname], Dear Principal [Surname], Dear Dr [Surname], Dear Professor [Surname],
Semi-formal — known / school context
Dear [First name], Dear fellow students, Dear members of [Club name], Dear [School name] community,
Speech / Talk — opening address
Good morning / afternoon, everyone. Distinguished guests, teachers and fellow students, Ladies and gentlemen, Dear members of the audience, Welcome, everyone.

Chips with a blue border are less common and will help you stand out from "Best regards".

Formal letters — very formal
Yours faithfully, Yours sincerely, Yours truly, Respectfully yours,

"Yours faithfully" → used when you don't know the recipient's name. "Yours sincerely" → when you addressed them by name.

Formal / semi-formal — professional but warmer
With appreciation, With gratitude, With warm regards, With the highest respect, With kind regards,
Semi-formal — friendly / school context
Best wishes, Warmly, Looking forward to hearing from you, Hoping for a positive response, I remain at your service,
Speech — closing remark
Thank you. Thank you for your time and attention. I sincerely hope this gives you food for thought. I look forward to seeing this vision become a reality. Together, we can make a difference. Thank you.
⚠ Avoid these — too common / penalised
Best regards, Kind regards, Best,

These are not "wrong" — examiners just see them in almost every script. Using a less common option signals better language range.
